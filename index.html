01 / THE SEGMENT THAT BLEEDS

Every new SMB customer is, on the math, destroying value.

SMB sits at a 1.2× LTV:CAC ratio against a 1.5× benchmark floor. Pair that with 78.6% net revenue retention and 2.2% monthly churn, and each cohort starts generating negative NPV inside 14 months. This is not a sales problem you can grow out of. It is a product and onboarding problem, and until early-tenure churn drops by at least four points, more SMB volume means faster value destruction.

02 / THE ENGINE

Enterprise isn't just retaining. It's compounding.

At €75.9M ARR, 106.3% net revenue retention, and a 4.2× LTV:CAC, Enterprise expands faster than it churns. CAC payback lands at 18 months, comfortably inside the 20-month benchmark. This is the cohort to protect and expand, not dilute by chasing logo volume in segments where the unit economics don't clear the bar.

03 / THE EARLY WARNING

The model sees churn coming 90 days before the cancellation email.

A logistic regression scoring all 1,461 active accounts hits an AUC of 0.781, ranking churn risk correctly in roughly 78% of account pairs. That is enough signal to move Customer Success from reactive firefighting to deliberate intervention. 231 Critical and High-priority accounts are flagged right now, representing €11.5M in ARR with a meaningful probability of churning before Q2.

04 / THE QUOTA ILLUSION

The best reps aren't closing the most. They're closing the healthiest.

Across 18 EMEA reps, average quota attainment reads an eye-watering 976%. But attainment is a lagging indicator. It rewards volume, not durability. The leading signal is portfolio health: the reps building books of high-retention, high-health accounts are the ones whose ARR will still be here in 18 months. Quota tells you who sold. Health tells you who sold something that lasts.

05 / THE EFFICIENCY VERDICT

Growing at 22% with 75% margins. Efficient, but not yet elite.

YoY ARR growth of 22% beats the 19% industry median. Gross margin of 75% clears the 72% median. But the Rule of 40 score lands at 28, short of the 40 that separates good SaaS from venture-grade SaaS. The path to 40 doesn't run through more spend. It runs through fixing SMB retention, which lifts both growth and margin at once.

Executive Takeaway

This business doesn't need to grow faster. It needs to grow cleaner. The headline ARR is healthy. The damage is hiding one tier down, in a segment the top-line number completely masks.

PulseMetrics simulates a €128M ARR EMEA-focused B2B SaaS company and builds the full analytical stack a CFO or CRO needs to run a quarterly business review: real-time health scoring, churn prediction, cohort analytics, unit economics, and rep-level performance intelligence.
